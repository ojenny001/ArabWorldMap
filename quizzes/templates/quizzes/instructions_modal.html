Overview

Talking Arab World Map focuses on not only improving students' Arabic language skills, but it also improves their knowledge on the culture and history of the 22 Arab countries. Students are able to select an Arab country from the world map. For each country, students can take a pre-assessment quiz and work through three learning phases as they improve. The first phase focuses on audio comprehension, the second phase focuses on conversations, and the third phase focuses on speaking & writing. Within each phase, students will learn and be tested on 5 categories: Location, Culture-Politics, Language-Religion, Population, and History-Economy. The three phases will take approximately 50 hours in order to achieve a 100% final score.

Phase 1

The student will watch a 1-3 minute video on the first category and then take a 5 question quiz about the video. If the student reaches a perfect score on the quiz, the student will be allowed to move on to the next category. The process wll repeat until the student has completed all 5 categories. After completing the categories, the student will be permitted to move on to Phase 2.

Phase 2

The student will read a short text about the first category and then have a conversation with a Western student about the text. Students will read a question posed by the Western student and will then be prompted to input a response. The audio output of the student's response will play. Students will continue to converse with the student until that category is complete. Once the student completes the conversation, the student will be allowed to move on to the next category. This process will repeat until the student has completed all 5 categories. After completing the categories, the student will be permitted to move on to Phase 3.

Phase 3

The student will provide written responses to 5 questions about the first category. Students will also upload audio recordings of them speaking their responses. After completing all 5 questions for that category, the student will be allowed to move onto the next category. This process will repeat until the student has completed all 5 categories. After completing the categories, the student will have successfully completed all three phases for that country.
